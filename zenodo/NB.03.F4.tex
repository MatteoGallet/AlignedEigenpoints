\section{Proposition}\label{proposition}}

    We know that \(\delta_2 = 0\) in general gives that
\[P_3 = (s_{14} s_{15} s_{22} - s_{12}^2 s_{45}) P_1 + s_{12} (s_{11} s_{45} - s_{14} s_{15}) P_2\]
hence, for some values of \(s_{ij}\), the point \(P_3\) is not defined.
Here we want to see when this happens. The conclusion is that \(P_3\) is
always uniquely defined, unless: 1) the points of the
\(V\)-configuration are such that \(s_{12}=0\), \(s_{14}=0\) (or
\(s_{13}=0\), \(s_{15}=0\), or\ldots). In this case the unique cubic is
